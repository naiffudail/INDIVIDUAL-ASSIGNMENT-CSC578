Stoneware made slow, on purpose

Every piece spends three weeks on the wheel, in the glaze, and in the kiln before it reaches your table. Join the list for first access to each new firing.

Why it holds up

Stoneware clay, fired hot

We fire every piece to cone 6, well past the point where clay turns to glass. The result is dense, chip-resistant ware that's safe in the dishwasher and microwave.

Packed the day it ships

Pieces leave the studio wrapped in the same straw and linen we use to cool them in the kiln, padded so nothing arrives chipped. Cracked in transit means a free replacement.

Checked before it leaves

Every cup and bowl is run under water and tapped for ring before packing. We're listening for the dull thud of a hidden crack, not just looking for one.

Watch a piece take shape

Three minutes, wheel to kiln: centering the clay, pulling the wall, and the glaze going on once it's bone dry.
